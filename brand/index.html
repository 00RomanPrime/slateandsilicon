Visual brand kit

Structured calm. Hard edges.

Slate & Silicon should feel like a quiet room with concrete walls: calm enough to decompress, severe enough to avoid wellness softness, and human enough to never become militaristic.

01

Brand essence

Cognitive recovery for people who lived online long enough to feel it. The visual system lives between softness and severity.

Too soft Wellness minimalism

Cozy, pretty, vague, emotionally diluted.

Target Structured calm

Editorial, restrained, tactile, slightly severe.

Too harsh Hacker control room

Cold, militaristic, unforgiving, non-human.

02

03

Color rules

The palette should never become decorative. It should feel like light, surface, signal, and restraint.

Do

  • Use black for 70–80% of the visual field.
  • Use amber only for signal, emphasis, or action.
  • Let bone white carry the emotional weight.
  • Use concrete gray to create hard section stops.

Do not

  • Use bright white backgrounds.
  • Use saturated digital colors.
  • Make amber feel cheerful.
  • Let blue become the dominant tech cue.
04

Typography

The type system should feel literary and controlled. Serif provides memory and humanity. Sans provides structure. Mono-style labels appear only as quiet interface residue.

Display serif

The internet changed us.

Body sans

You do not need a better system. You need enough quiet to notice what the system did to you.

Functional accent

01 — Recognition sequence

Behavior

No excessive bolding. No decorative all-caps paragraphs. No motivational poster scale. Headlines should feel written, not performed.

05

Layout system

Use hard dividers, narrow reading widths, asymmetry, and occasional compression. The page should breathe, then stop abruptly enough to feel structured.

06

Visual language

The visual world is analog material under digital pressure. Use interface fragments, concrete texture, paper, matte surfaces, screen residue, and hard framing.

Silence felt off.

08

Do / do not

This system fails when it becomes either wellness-soft or tech-severe. Stay in the tension.

Do

  • Use restraint everywhere.
  • Let silence and spacing carry meaning.
  • Keep the design slightly unresolved.
  • Make it feel familiar before it feels beautiful.

Do not

  • Over-design the recovery.
  • Use motivational language.
  • Make the brutalism aggressive.
  • Let the brand become non-human.

This isn’t meant to look good. It’s meant to feel familiar.
