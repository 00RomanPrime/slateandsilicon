Where Does Your
Phone Sleep?

The seventh transmission from The Dispatch. Not a trick question. The answer tells you more than you'd think.

Signal Seven // DIAG-03

Where does your phone sleep?

Not a trick question. Think about it for a second before you answer. Nightstand. Under the pillow. Charging on the floor within arm's reach of the bed. However you answered, there's a decent chance the answer is: close enough to touch without sitting up.

This week's diagnostic is smaller than last time, and it only takes one night.

Put the phone somewhere else. Not off. Somewhere else. Another room, a bag, a drawer you'd have to get out of bed for. Then just notice what happens.

Most people report the same three things, in this order: a check for it that doesn't land on anything, because it's not there. A longer stretch of just lying there before sleep actually shows up. And a specific kind of morning: waking up before checking anything, instead of the other way around.

That's DIAG-03. The overnight test. It's not about sleep hygiene. It's about finding out how much of your night was actually being run by something in the next room.
